My **favourite theorem** is the **Aziz-Mackenzie Theorem** (Link to [Wikipedia entry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Envy-free_cake-cutting#Short_history) on *Envy-free Cake-Cutting*).

<Callout type="info" title="Aziz-Mackenzie Theorem" collapsible collapsed>
    Proven in $2016$, by `Haris Aziz` and `Simon Mackenzie`, this theorem says that:
    > There exists an [envy-free cake-cutting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Envy-free_cake-cutting) protocol with a bounded number of steps for any number of people.

    The reason I find this very interesting is because, firstly, this had been unsolved for decades! And the number of steps, while bounded, is still absolutely mind-bogglingly humongous.

    They basically presented a discrete envy-free protocol that requires at most $n^{n^{n^{n^{n^n}}}}$ (or more nicely, $n\uparrow\uparrow 6$) many queries.

    This is just a great example of things escaping intuition just due to sheer numbers, and while this protocol isn't useful at the slightest in practice, I find it to be my favourite owing to this depiction and its theoretical significance.
</Callout>
